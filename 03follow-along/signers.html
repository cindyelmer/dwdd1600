Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence and one of the most prominent Founding Fathers. He later became the third President of the United States.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence and a key member of the Continental Congress. He would later become the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned polymath, diplomat, and statesman, Franklin was one of the most well-known figures in colonial America. He played a critical role in securing French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock
The president of the Continental Congress at the time of the Declarationâ€™s signing, Hancock is famous for his bold and prominent signature, which became symbolic of American independence.
Samuel Adams
A strong and vocal leader in the American Revolution, Samuel Adams was crucial in organizing resistance to British rule and played a significant role in the events leading up to the signing of the Declaration.
Roger Sherman
A lawyer and statesman from Connecticut, Sherman was the only person to sign all four major American documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Richard Henry Lee
A prominent Virginian who proposed the resolution for independence in June 1776, which ultimately led to the Declaration being written and adopted.
Francis Lewis
A merchant and political leader from New York, he was a vocal supporter of independence and was a signer of the Declaration.

These men, among others, were integral in the movement for American independence and in the formal declaration of that independence on July 4, 1776.
